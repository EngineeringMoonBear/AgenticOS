Summary (L0)

Operational note describing the 21-day rotation cadence across zones A through D. Establishes weighting rules for milking-parlor proximity and an annual reassessment trigger after spring soil tests.

Overview (L1)

The herd moves through four contiguous paddocks on a 21-day base cadence. Zone A (closest to the milking parlor) is weighted ×1.3 — the cows return there more often during peak lactation to reduce walking distance. Zones B–D are revisited on a flat schedule unless the soil test in early March flags P deficiency in any zone.

During the dry months (typically July 15 to August 30), cadence stretches to 28 days and a supplemental hay ration is delivered each evening on the home paddock. The signal to extend is sustained pasture height below 4 inches on any zone for three consecutive days.

Decision triggers

  • Soil test P < 25 ppm in any zone → re-weight that zone down by ×0.7 for one cycle.
  • Sustained pasture height < 4 in. for 3 days → extend cadence to 28 days, begin hay supplementation.
  • Heavy rain event > 1.5 in./24h → defer rotation 24h to prevent compaction.
